The Grand Egyptian Museum

The Grand Egyptian Museum in Giza is one of the famous tourist places in Egypt. This amazing museum is located near the Pyramids of Giza and houses 136,000 ancient artifacts from the Pharaonic civilization. The Grand Egyptian Museum is a prominent site among Egypt's historical tourist destinations. It is characterized by its modern architectural style and its cost of 550 million dollars. Moreover, inside the museum, there is a children's education center, temporary exhibition halls, a cinema, and a conference center. The museum is set to offer a special and unique experience to its visitors, coinciding with the events at the Giza Pyramids for the year 2023.

Where is the Museum?

Salah El-Din Al-Ayyubi Citadel

The Salah El-Din Al-Ayyubi Citadel in Cairo is one of Cairo's landmarks of historical value that bears witness to its strength and fortification. It is considered one of the most beautiful architectural models and one of the most famous tourist attractions for those planning a Cairo tourism experience. Opened in 1176, the citadel is distinguished by many features such as its unique location on a high hill and its magnificent structure.

The citadel features various exhibits that you will find in the citadel museum, including ceramic works, ancient coins, artifacts, pottery vessels, and maritime artifacts. You will be amazed by the inscriptions and decorations on the walls of the citadel, some dating back to the Middle Ages. The citadel also served as a weapons store, housing different types of weapons like rifles, cannons, swords, daggers, shields, and spears.

You can also visit the mosques inside the citadel, such as the stunning Mosque of Muhammad Ali and the Al-Nasir Muhammad Ibn Qalawun Mosque. It will be lovely to spend some outdoor time as the citadel is surrounded by a beautiful garden with seating areas, fountains, and some wonderful sculptures.

Where is the citadel?

Cairo Tower

If you are looking to visit one of the tourist spots in Cairo along the Nile, then Cairo Tower is your perfect destination! It is considered one of the most beautiful places to go out in Cairo on the Nile and one of Egypt's most important landmarks. It is designed in the shape of the Egyptian lotus flower. At Cairo Tower, one of Egypt’s prominent landmarks, you will climb 16 floors reaching a height of 187 meters to enjoy one of the most beautiful panoramic views of Greater Cairo. The best time to visit Cairo Tower is during sunset, as the view is incredibly magical at that time.

Where is Cairo Tower?

Khan El-Khalili

Everyone wanting to experience tourism in Cairo seeks to enjoy the journey in various ways, such as visiting different attractions and exploring Cairo’s renowned markets. Specifically, when talking about a distinguished shopping experience in this city, Khan El-Khalili Bazaar in Cairo will ensure you a fun shopping adventure. It is a famous historical market that many people visit.

Khan El-Khalili competes with many other markets in the city because it offers everything visitors look for. The market includes shops specializing in selling shoes and shops with a beautiful variety of bags.

You will find a unique collection of handicrafts at Khan El-Khalili, and you will surely be fascinated by how skillful the artisans are. You might even want to buy some items like brassware and pottery vessels.

Visitors will not ignore buying a few special pieces that will serve as souvenirs of the visit. Surely, you will do the same as you will find shops selling antiques and gift shops with eye-catching products.

Where is Khan El-Khalili?

Al-Muizz Street

One of the most important streets in Old Cairo, you will feel as if you are inside an open museum of magnificent Islamic architecture and art. This great street includes the Gate of Victory at its entrance, which was one of the gates of Fatimid Cairo and is considered a unique architectural masterpiece. It also includes a large number of historical mosques such as Al-Hakim Bi-Amr Allah Mosque, the School and Dome of Najm Al-Din Ayyub, and many other awe-inspiring landmarks of great size and beauty.

Where is Al-Muizz Street?

Pyramids of Giza

The Pyramids of Giza are one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World and are considered among the most important tourist attractions in Egypt. The Giza Plateau includes three main pyramids: Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure, which were built as royal tombs around 4,500 years ago. The Great Pyramid of Khufu is the largest and is considered an architectural marvel.

The pyramids attract millions of tourists annually to admire their magnificence and mystery. In addition to the pyramids, visitors can also see the Sphinx, the giant statue with the body of a lion and the head of a pharaoh, which symbolizes strength and wisdom.

Where are the Pyramids?

The National Museum of Egyptian Civilization

One of the best museums in Old Cairo, overlooking Ain El-Sira Lake, it spans an area of 33.5 acres and houses 50,000 artifacts that narrate the stages of the development of Egyptian civilization. It showcases the achievements of the Egyptian people in various fields of life since the dawn of history. It also contains models, photographs, manuscripts, as well as oil paintings, artistic artifacts, and relics from the Stone Age, Pharaonic, Roman, Greek, as well as Coptic and modern civilization.

Where is the National Museum located?

The Pharaonic Village

If you are an adventure lover and fascinated by history, you will wish to visit this village today before tomorrow. It is undoubtedly one of the most beautiful tourist attractions in Giza, as this village makes you feel as if you are in the Pharaonic era and not the modern era! How is that? Well, follow the next lines to find out. The idea of this village was built by the creative scientist Dr. Hassan Ragab, and the idea was to build a completely natural and real village, with real people dressed in Pharaonic clothing, representing life in ancient Egyptian times during the Pharaonic era. This village also has 5000 trees, a garden, a market, as well as Pharaonic houses by the Nile, boats, and much more.

Where is the Pharaonic Village located?

Zoo

The Giza Zoo has gained wide fame to become what it is today as one of Cairo's most prominent gardens. For visitors, it is not just a source of entertainment but has become a place that introduces them to a variety of animals as well as different species of animals. This garden is one of the most important tourist spots in Cairo, attracting everyone to visit it. Therefore, it has been classified among the top tourist attractions in Cairo for families and is also one of the best destinations for those looking for tourist places in Cairo for children.

Where is the Zoo located?

Qaitbay Citadel

Qaitbay Citadel is one of the most important tourist attractions in Alexandria. Its history dates back to the 15th century and it was built to protect the city from invasions coming through the Mediterranean Sea. The citadel is a significant cultural landmark in the city and attracts tourists from all over the world.

Qaitbay Citadel was listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2007 due to its historical and cultural importance. It is considered one of the most important tourist attractions in Egypt, offering stunning views of the city and the Mediterranean Sea. It also hosts a variety of exhibitions and cultural events.

Where is Qaitbay Citadel?

Bibliotheca Alexandrina

The Bibliotheca Alexandrina is one of the most important cultural landmarks in Alexandria. It was established in 2002 and is considered a revival of the ancient library that once stood in the same location in antiquity.

The library is one of the largest in the world, housing nearly 8 million books. It includes various departments for knowledge, arts, sciences, and different cultures. Additionally, it contains research centers and laboratories that support education and innovation.

Where is the Bibliotheca Alexandrina?

Roman Theater

The Roman Theater is located in Kom El-Dikka. It is one of the Roman-era relics and was built in the early 4th century AD. It was discovered by chance while searching for the tomb of “Alexander the Great.” Excavation took around 30 years. The theater consists of 13 marble steps arranged in a U-shape. The steps were numbered in Greek from bottom to top to organize the seating process and could accommodate about 600 people.

Where is the Roman Theater?

Marine Biology Museum

The Marine Biology Museum is located on Qaitbay Street and includes many diverse marine animals and plants such as shells, fish, turtles, plants, and sponges that live in Egyptian lakes and seas, especially the rare ones.

It also includes some types of plants and shells found in both seawater and river water. The museum has a full laboratory for studying the marine organisms it houses. Part of the museum features some preserved fish and marine creatures, such as the skeleton of a shark and a model of a mermaid from old myths.

Where is the Marine Biology Museum?

Elsawary Pillar

It is considered one of the most important attractions and tourist sites in Alexandria and among the most important in Egypt. Visitors flock to see its impressive construction and towering height. Elsawary's Pillar dates back to the Roman era and was built in honor of Emperor Diocletian, who ended the rebellion led by “Achilles” and restored peace to the city.

It was formerly known as the “Column of the Masts” due to the tall ship masts, but the name later changed to Elsawary's Pillar.” It is built from red granite and stands at a height of 20.75 meters.

Where is Elsawary Pillar?

Karnak Temple

Karnak Temple is a must-visit when traveling to Luxor. This magnificent temple complex stands as a testament to the grandeur of ancient Egyptian architecture and history. As you wander among the colossal columns, towering obelisks, and intricate hieroglyphs, you can't help but feel transported back in time. The sheer scale of the site is astounding, and the detailed carvings and paintings are breathtaking

Where is Karnak Temple ?

Luxor Museum

Despite its smaller size, the Luxor Museum is beautifully organized with a rich collection of artifacts that offer fascinating insights into ancient history. The interior layout is arranged in a way that makes it easy to navigate the exhibits, with accurate explanatory panels that help visitors understand the historical context of each piece.

Where is the Luxor Museum?

The temple of Hatshepsut

Hatshepsut Temple: A Marvel of Ancient Egyptian Architecture The Hatshepsut Temple is an astonishing architectural marvel. Situated against dramatic cliffs, it boasts a stunning and unique design. The temple's history, grandeur, and breathtaking surroundings make it a must-visit destination in Egypt, offering a glimpse into the incredible achievements of an Egyptian pharaoh

Where is The temple of Hatshepsut?

valley of the kings

The museum impressively displays the tombs of the pharaohs, offering visitors a unique experience. There are a total of five tomb chambers, but each ticket allows entry to only three of them. Each chamber has a distinct atmosphere that captivates visitors while highlighting the mysterious world of the pharaohs

Where is valley of the kings?

The Ramesseum

The Ramesseum, the mortuary temple of Pharaoh Ramesses II, stands as a poignant reminder of the impermanence of power and the passage of time. Once a magnificent edifice, the Ramesseum has suffered significant damage over the centuries. Despite its current state, it remains well worth a visit, especially if you enjoy a quieter, more secluded experience away from the crowds

Where is The Ramesseum?

Philae Temple

Philae Temple. It's a beautiful ancient Egyptian temple complex that was dedicated primarily to the goddess Isis. It's famously known for being meticulously relocated, stone by stone, from its original island of Philae to the nearby Agilkia Island in the 1960s to save it from being submerged by the Aswan High Dam.

Where is Philae Temple ?

Nubian Museum

A museum established by UNESCO in Egypt to display artifacts related to ancient Nubian civilization. It also includes information about the history of Nubia from prehistoric times to the present day, along with an overview of important Nubian customs and traditions.

Where is the Luxor Museum?

The Abu Simbel temples

The Abu Simbel temples are considered among the most important tourist attractions in Aswan. They were constructed by Ramses II for himself and his wife. The Great Temple features four colossal statues of King Ramses II at its facade, with smaller statues representing the King's children, his wife, and his mother at their feet.

Where is The Abu Simbel temples?

El Gouna

El Gouna is characterized by golden sandy beaches and turquoise Red Sea waters, with numerous small islands adding to the natural beauty.

Where is El Gouna ?

Hurghada Grand Aquarium

An aquatic life exhibition featuring freshwater and marine creatures, with an underwater tunnel, a Bedouin life exhibit, a zoo, and a restaurant.

Where is Hurghada Grand Aquarium?

Sand City Hurghada

An open-air museum featuring numerous historical and pictorial sand sculptures by international artists.

Where is Sand City Hurghada?

Siwa Oasis

Siwa vast expanses are dotted with wells and springs used for irrigation, drinking, bottling natural water, and therapy. It features four major lakes, and several archaeological sites have been discovered there, such as the Temple of Amun.

Where is Siwa Oasis ?

Ramses Family Mall

If you are looking for a distinctive souvenir that preserves the memory of your trip to the Egyptian city of Dahab, you should go to Ramses Family Mall, it is a large store that provides its customers with a range of the best local products that can be purchased as souvenirs, such as perfumes, natural oils, handicrafts, souvenirs, jewelry, jewelry and pharaonic papyrus On which you can print whatever images, symbols, letters, etc..

Where is Ramses Family Mall ?

Eel garden view dahab

Eel Garden (Diving Spot): This is a renowned diving location in Dahab, known for its large colony of garden eels. The eels live in burrows on the sandy sea floor and can be seen waving their heads in the current. The spot gets its name from these numerous garden eels. You can access it by wading across a reef shelf, entering a channel, and passing over a coral ridge before descending to the eels.

Where is Eel garden view dahab ?
